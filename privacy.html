Scope of This Privacy Policy

Musclog is made available through both an application and a public website. Because those two services operate differently, the applicable privacy practices are summarized separately below and described in more detail throughout this Privacy Policy.

Within the Musclog Application

The Musclog application is designed primarily as a local-first product. Most user data remains stored on your device and is not transmitted to our servers.

  • ›All data you enter into the application is stored locally on your device.
  • ›The application does not require an account and does not provide cloud synchronization.
  • ›We do not have access to your health or fitness data stored within the application.
  • ›Sensitive data stored by the application is encrypted at rest using AES.

On This Website

The website processes a narrower set of data, primarily relating to website operation, language preference, analytics, and cookie consent management.

  • ›This website uses Vercel Analytics to measure anonymous, cookieless traffic data without requiring consent.
  • ›This website uses Google Analytics 4 only after you provide explicit consent through the cookie banner.
  • ›Your language preference is stored locally in your browser and is not transmitted to our servers.
  • ›Your cookie consent choice is stored locally in your browser and is not transmitted to our servers.

Optional AI Features and Third-Party LLM Providers

The Musclog application can be used fully offline, except for optional requests to external nutrition data sources. By default, Musclog does not send your workout, nutrition, health, or profile data to our servers.

Musclog also offers optional AI features that require you to configure your own provider, such as OpenAI, Google Gemini, Apple Intelligence on iOS, or a local/self-hosted LLM endpoint. These features are disabled unless you explicitly enable them.

If you choose to use an AI feature, the data that you decide to submit will be sent to the provider or endpoint you configure. If you use a self-hosted local model under your own control, that data may remain within your own environment. If you use a third-party hosted endpoint, including a hosted local-model service, that provider may process the data in accordance with its own terms and privacy practices. Musclog does not control how external providers process data you send to them.

Musclog may also offer a Musclog free-tier LLM option. When you use that option, your request is routed through Cloudflare AI Gateway and then forwarded using a Musclog-managed free-tier OpenAI or Google Gemini API key. If you choose to use this feature, you should understand that your request may be processed through Cloudflare and by the downstream model provider selected by Musclog for that request.

At present, Musclog supports OpenAI, Google Gemini, Apple Intelligence on iOS, compatible local or self-hosted LLM endpoints, and the Musclog free-tier LLM route. You should review the applicable privacy policies before enabling third-party providers or accepting the free-tier option, including: Cloudflare Privacy Policy, OpenAI Privacy Policy, Google Privacy Policy, and Apple Privacy Policy.

This website also uses Google Analytics 4 (GA4), a service provided by Google LLC, to collect anonymized data about how visitors interact with the site. Analytics cookies are only set after you explicitly consent by clicking "Accept" in the cookie banner.

When you consent to GA4, Google Analytics may collect:

  • ›Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • ›General geographic location (country or city level, not precise location)
  • ›Browser type, device type, and operating system
  • ›Referring website or search engine

Legal basis for GA4: Consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)). You may withdraw your consent at any time by clicking "Cookie settings" in the page footer. Withdrawal of consent will prevent any further collection and does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

GA4 data is retained in Google Analytics for 14 months by default. Google acts as a data processor under a Data Processing Agreement. For more information on how Google handles data, see the Google Privacy Policy.

Your consent choice is stored locally in your browser (localStorage) and is never sent to our servers. It is used solely to remember your preference between visits.
